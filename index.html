Executive Summary

I build things that work - systems, organizations, and governance frameworks that hold up under pressure.

My career has moved across three domains that most people keep separate: enterprise infrastructure engineering, environmental entrepreneurship, and technology risk & governance. That breadth is not a detour. It is the point.

I started by getting my hands deep into enterprise technology, designing and implementing Cisco-based network and infrastructure environments, learning how systems are actually built, where they break, and what it takes to keep them running at scale. That foundation changed how I see risk. Not as a checklist, but as a systems problem.

In parallel, I built an award-winning sustainability startup from the ground up, pioneering waste upcycling and circular economy solutions that turned post-consumer materials into construction value. I ran the technology, the operations, the compliance considerations, and the strategy. Building something real, under constraints, with accountability for outcomes, that is a different education than any classroom offers.

What ties it all together is a systems-level instinct: the ability to understand how the pieces connect, where pressure concentrates, and what it takes to make complex environments more resilient, efficient, and sustainable, whether that is a network, an organization, or a supply chain.

I hold a Master of Engineering Management from Michigan Technological University, and certifications in CISA, CISM, CRISC, and AAISM, including one of the first credentials in AI systems management, a space I am actively growing into.

I am based in the United States, eager to contribute, and at my best in environments that value people who think across disciplines and deliver with precision.

I built a Compliance Control Center in under 6 hours

Not because I'm a great coder (my coding skills are basic), but because I deeply understand compliance problems and take initiative without waiting to be told.

This was about proving one thing:

Skills should make work easier — not harder. That's what automation is for.

The solution tackles one of the biggest compliance pain points organizations face:

  • Employee training & tracking - Assign training, notify employees and supervisors, track completion, run assessments, and auto-generate certificates — all in one flow.
  • Third-party / vendor risk management - Register vendors, assign risk ratings, upload certifications (e.g., SOC 2), track expiry dates, assign accountability, and get automatic alerts.
  • Control monitoring & RACI - Know who owns what. See compliance status at a glance.
  • Reporting - Generate audit-ready reports in minutes.

All built in less than 6 hours.

Because this isn't about knowing how to code. It's about knowing the problem and designing the right solution.
